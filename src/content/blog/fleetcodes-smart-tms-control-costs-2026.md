## The Six Cost Levers Fleetcodes Actively Controls

### 1. Fuel — Your Single Biggest Variable Cost

Fuel typically represents 35–40% of total operating costs for an Indian logistics fleet. It's also the cost most susceptible to waste through driver behaviour, poor route planning, and vehicle inefficiency.

Fleetcodes addresses fuel costs from multiple angles:

- **Route optimisation** — shortest practical routes are suggested at dispatch, reducing unnecessary kilometres
- **Driver behaviour monitoring** — excessive idling, harsh braking, and speeding (all of which increase fuel consumption significantly) are flagged via **real-time tracking** integration
- **Fuel consumption tracking per vehicle** — Fleetcodes builds a fuel efficiency profile for every vehicle, making it immediately visible when a truck's consumption spikes above its baseline
- **Fuel expense validation** — driver-submitted fuel receipts are cross-checked against actual distance travelled, preventing inflated claims

For a 100-vehicle fleet, reducing fuel waste by even 8% represents significant annual savings — often in the range of ₹50–80 lakhs depending on fleet size and route mix.

### 2. Empty Miles — Revenue You're Not Earning

As covered in depth in our guide on [deadhead miles](https://www.fleetcodes.com), empty return legs are one of the most expensive and most addressable cost items in **fleet management shipping**.

Fleetcodes tracks empty vs. loaded km per vehicle and per route, giving dispatchers real-time visibility into trucks finishing deliveries with no return load assigned — and the tools to fill those gaps before the truck leaves for base.

### 3. Maintenance — Reactive vs. Preventive

A truck that breaks down on a highway doesn't just cost repair money — it costs customer confidence, delays the load, and creates overtime or alternative vehicle costs to recover the delivery.

Fleetcodes manages preventive maintenance schedules for every vehicle in the fleet — tracking km since last service, scheduled service dates, and component-level maintenance history. When a service is due, the system generates an alert before the truck goes out on a run — not after it breaks down.

**Preventive maintenance typically costs 3–4x less than reactive repair**, and Fleetcodes makes it the default rather than the exception for **fleet mgmt** operations of any size.

### 4. Driver Costs — Wages, Advances, and Overtime

Driver costs are the second-largest expense item for most logistics businesses, and also one of the most difficult to manage without an integrated system.

Fleetcodes tracks every driver's trip assignments, hours worked, advances taken, and expense claims — giving the HR and accounts team a clean, auditable record that eliminates the guesswork from settlement calculations. Overtime is flagged automatically when trip scheduling creates it unnecessarily, allowing dispatchers to redistribute loads more evenly.

### 5. Toll and Detention Charges — The Invisible Cost Adds

Toll costs on long-haul routes are significant and often poorly tracked in manual systems. Detention charges — for trucks waiting beyond agreed loading/unloading windows — are frequently disputed and sometimes simply not billed.

Fleetcodes captures toll costs per trip from driver app inputs and cross-references them with route data. Detention time is automatically tracked against agreed loading windows, and the billing engine flags when charges should be applied to customers.

For **fleet management india private limited** businesses running multi-state operations, proper toll and detention tracking alone can recover meaningful revenue that was previously being absorbed as an operational loss.

### 6. Asset Utilisation — The Cost of Underperforming Vehicles

Every vehicle in your fleet represents a capital investment. A truck that runs only 15 days a month, or that consistently returns with empty km, is delivering poor return on that investment.

Fleetcodes' asset utilisation dashboard shows you exactly how each vehicle is performing — km operated, load ratio, revenue generated, and cost incurred — giving fleet managers the data to make decisions about fleet composition, vehicle allocation, and eventual replacement timing.
