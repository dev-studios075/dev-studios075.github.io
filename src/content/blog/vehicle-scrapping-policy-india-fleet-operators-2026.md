# Vehicle Scrapping Policy India: What Fleet Operators Must Know in 2026

**By Team Fleetcodes | May 2026 | 5 Min Read**

---

> **India's Vehicle Scrappage Policy has introduced mandatory end-of-life timelines for commercial vehicles — and financial incentives that make early scrapping commercially rational. For fleet operators running older vehicles, 2026 is the year to act strategically rather than reactively.**

---

## What the Policy Says: Key Timelines for Commercial Vehicles

Under the Vehicle Scrappage Policy (notified 2021, progressively implemented):

**Commercial vehicles 15+ years old** must undergo mandatory fitness retesting to continue operating. The Certificate of Fitness (CF) renewal process for these vehicles has been significantly tightened — with stringent emission, safety, and roadworthiness criteria that older vehicles frequently fail.

In practice: a commercial vehicle that reaches 15 years without passing the enhanced CF inspection cannot legally continue in revenue-earning service. Many pre-BS4 vehicles are not economically viable to upgrade to meet current standards.

**The MoRTH timeline in 2026:**
- Government-owned commercial vehicles older than 15 years: already phased out
- Private commercial vehicles (freight): enhanced CF testing from 15 years; effectively end-of-practical-life for pre-BS4 fleet
- Enhanced Automated Testing Stations (CATS) are expanding nationally — making enforcement more systematic

## The Financial Incentives: Why Scrapping Makes Commercial Sense

The policy creates a positive economic case for scrapping beyond just compliance:

**Certificate of Deposit (Scrappage Certificate):**
When a vehicle is scrapped at a Registered Vehicle Scrapping Facility (RVSF), the owner receives a Certificate of Deposit. This certificate entitles you to:

- **Waiver of registration fees** on the new vehicle purchased against it (saves Rs 15,000-50,000 depending on vehicle class)
- **5% discount on new vehicle purchase** from manufacturers who have joined the scheme
- **Road tax concession** in most states for vehicles purchased against a scrapping certificate

**Scrap value itself:**
Depending on vehicle weight and scrap metal rates, a scrapped commercial vehicle generates Rs 50,000-2,00,000+ in raw scrap value. This is cash recovered from what would otherwise be a depreciating liability.

**Insurance savings:**
Older vehicles carry higher insurance premiums due to age and higher claim probability. Replacing a 15-year-old vehicle with a new one reduces insurance costs.

## How to Identify Candidates in Your Fleet

Not all old vehicles are equal scrapping candidates. Use these criteria:

**Scrap now:**
- Pre-BS4 vehicles approaching or past 15 years
- Vehicles whose annual maintenance cost exceeds 30% of current market value
- Vehicles failing or likely to fail enhanced CF inspection
- Vehicles with high fuel consumption relative to fleet baseline (Fleetcodes identifies these automatically via per-vehicle consumption tracking)

**Retain or refurbish:**
- BS4+ vehicles with well-documented maintenance history
- Vehicles in high-utilisation roles where replacement disrupts operations
- Vehicles with recent major investment (engine rebuild, major overhaul)

Scrapping is most valuable when coordinated with a fleet renewal plan:

## FAQs

**At what age must commercial vehicles be scrapped in India?**
Commercial vehicles 15 years or older must pass enhanced fitness testing at Automated Testing Stations to continue operating. Practically, most pre-BS4 vehicles (manufactured before April 2017) do not meet current emission and safety standards and are effectively at end-of-life under the enhanced testing regime.

**What incentives does the scrapping certificate provide?**
A Certificate of Deposit from a Registered Vehicle Scrapping Facility entitles the owner to registration fee waiver on a replacement vehicle, a 5% new vehicle purchase discount from participating manufacturers, and road tax concessions in most states.
