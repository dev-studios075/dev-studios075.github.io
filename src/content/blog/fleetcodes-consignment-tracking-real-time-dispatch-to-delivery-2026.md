## How Consignment Tracking Works in Fleetcodes

### At Booking

When a trip is created, the consignment record is established — consignor, consignee, goods description, weight, rate card reference, and e-way bill. The shipper with customer portal access sees "booking confirmed, vehicle assigned" before the truck moves.

### At Departure

Driver marks departure in the Fleetcodes app. Pre-loading checklist confirms the consignment is on board. Status updates to "in transit" with a departure timestamp and live GPS position.

From this moment, position updates every 30-60 seconds. Dispatcher, operations manager, and shipper see the same live picture simultaneously.

### During the Journey

The consignment is shown with operational context — not just a vehicle dot on a map:

- **Current location:** GPS-updated continuously
- **Dynamic ETA:** Recalculated in real time from current position and historical route performance
- **Route adherence:** Deviation from the planned route flagged automatically
- **Exception alerts:** Extended stop, geofence breach, idle beyond threshold — each triggers a status alert

### At Delivery

Driver completes delivery in the Fleetcodes app:
- Recipient digital signature
- Photo of delivered goods
- Geotag and timestamp
- Any exceptions noted (partial delivery, damaged item, refusal)

Digital POD is available to dispatcher, operations manager, and shipper simultaneously — the moment the driver confirms. Invoice generation triggers automatically. Consignment status updates to "delivered" with a complete timestamped audit trail.
