## The 5 Places Double Entry Destroys Operational Efficiency

### 1. Order Entry vs. Dispatch
Sales or customer service logs an order. The dispatcher re-enters it to create a trip. One field entered differently — a weight, a pickup time, a destination — and the entire delivery chain is off.

### 2. Trip Completion vs. Billing
The driver marks a delivery complete. The billing team waits for a physical POD before raising an invoice. Days pass. Cash flow suffers. With integrated **fleet management shipping** workflows, digital POD confirmation triggers the invoice automatically.

### 3. Driver Logs vs. Payroll
Drivers submit trip sheets. HR or accounts manually tallies hours, kilometers, and allowances. Errors are common. Disputes are frequent. Integrated **fleet mgmt** systems pull trip data directly into settlement calculations — zero re-entry.

### 4. GPS Data vs. Operations Reports
Your real-time tracking system knows exactly where every vehicle is and how long each trip took. But if that data doesn't feed into your TMS, your operations team is still building reports manually from multiple sources.

### 5. Customer Rate Cards vs. Invoices
Different customers have different rate agreements. Applying the right rate manually on every invoice is slow and error-prone. Integrated transport management system software applies rates automatically based on route, load type, and customer contract.
