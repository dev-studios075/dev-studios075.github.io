## What Are Deadhead Miles — and Why Should You Care?

Deadhead miles (also called empty miles) are the kilometres a truck travels without a paying load — typically on the return leg after a delivery, or when repositioning a vehicle to the next pickup.

For most fleet operators, deadhead is accepted as an unavoidable cost of doing business. But here's the uncomfortable truth:

**The average Indian logistics fleet runs 25–35% of total kilometres empty.**

On a fleet of 50 trucks averaging 10,000 km/month each, that's up to **1,75,000 km per month of completely unmonetised movement** — burning fuel, wearing down vehicles, and paying driver wages for zero revenue.

If your **fleet management software** isn't actively tracking and reducing deadhead, you're bleeding profit and you may not even know exactly how much.

## Why Deadhead Is an Especially Serious Problem in Indian Logistics

India's logistics network presents unique challenges that make deadhead miles worse than in many other markets:

- **Hub-and-spoke imbalance** — freight flows heavily from manufacturing hubs (Pune, Surat, Ludhiana) to distribution cities, but return loads are scarce on the same lanes
- **Fragmented load booking** — without integrated **transport management system software**, dispatchers book loads in isolation without visibility into nearby return opportunities
- **Poor real-time tracking integration** — if your GPS data and your load management system don't talk to each other, dispatchers can't see which trucks are finishing nearby
- **Last-minute booking culture** — many shippers in India confirm loads late, leaving fleet operators unable to plan return trips effectively

The result: trucks that could be earning on the return leg are deadheading back to base. Every trip. Every day.

## The Dispatch Behaviour That Makes Deadhead Worse

Beyond the software, there are operational habits that compound the deadhead problem — and that integrated **fleet management shipping** tools help correct:

**Siloed dispatching:** When each dispatcher manages their own set of trucks without visibility into the wider fleet, opportunities to match trucks with nearby return loads are missed constantly.

**Manual status updates:** If drivers call in their status rather than updating a connected app, dispatchers are always working with incomplete, delayed information — making proactive load matching impossible.

**No cost visibility per load:** When dispatchers don't see the cost of empty return legs, there's no urgency to fill them. Fleetcodes surfaces the cost of each empty trip in real time, creating accountability.
