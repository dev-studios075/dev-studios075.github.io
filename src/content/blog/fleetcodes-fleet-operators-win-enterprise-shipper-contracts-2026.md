## What Enterprise Shippers Actually Require from Logistics Partners

When a large FMCG company, pharmaceutical manufacturer, or e-commerce platform evaluates a logistics vendor, they are not primarily comparing rates. They are running a qualification process — assessing whether the carrier can meet operational, compliance, and service standards that protect the shipper's own supply chain performance.

The criteria that enterprise shippers consistently evaluate in 2026:

### 1. Real-Time Shipment Visibility

Enterprise supply chain managers need to know where their freight is — not when they call the transporter, but continuously, in a system they control.

This means a customer-facing tracking portal with live vehicle position, dynamic ETA, delivery milestone status, and proactive exception alerts. A dispatcher who answers status calls manually is not meeting this requirement. A customer portal that answers those questions automatically — 24 hours a day, without human intervention — is.

[Fleetcodes](https://www.fleetcodes.com/) provides a shipper portal where enterprise customers access live consignment tracking, milestone updates, and delivery confirmation for every load — without the transporter's team needing to be involved.

### 2. Digital Documentation Chain

Enterprise shippers are audited by their own customers, by regulators, and in pharma by international quality authorities. They need their logistics partners to maintain complete, digital, searchable documentation for every consignment: bilty, e-way bill, digital POD with photo and timestamp, driver details, vehicle compliance records.

Paper-based documentation fails this requirement. A dispute about whether a delivery occurred, what condition goods were in at delivery, or what time a vehicle arrived at a customer dock — all of these are resolved in seconds from Fleetcodes digital records. Without digital records, they become contested disputes that damage the commercial relationship.

### 3. Compliance Reliability

An enterprise shipper whose outbound freight is stopped at a checkpoint because their carrier's vehicle has an expired fitness certificate is not just inconvenienced. It affects their own delivery commitments, potentially triggers SLA penalties, and reflects on their procurement decision.

Fleetcodes tracks vehicle document expiry (fitness certificate, insurance, PUC, permits) and driver licence validity per vehicle — with advance alerts ensuring nothing expires unnoticed. Enterprise shippers increasingly ask vendors to demonstrate this compliance tracking capability during the qualification process.

### 4. Billing Accuracy and Speed

Enterprise shipper accounts teams process hundreds or thousands of freight invoices monthly. Inaccurate invoices — wrong rates, missing surcharges, incorrect GST treatment — create matching failures, payment holds, and disputes that consume accounts payable time on both sides.

The standard enterprise shippers are moving toward: same-day invoicing from confirmed digital POD, with correct rate card application and GST structure. Manual billing processes that produce invoices 3-7 days after delivery, with rate errors that require correction cycles, do not meet this standard.

Fleetcodes generates invoices automatically from digital POD confirmation — applying the customer's stored rate card, including applicable surcharges, and producing a GST-compliant invoice within minutes of delivery confirmation.

### 5. Performance Reporting

Enterprise procurement teams evaluate logistics vendor performance quarterly — against agreed KPIs including on-time delivery rate, billing dispute rate, cargo damage rate, and POD compliance rate.

A vendor who can produce this data from a connected fleet management system, with timestamps and GPS verification, is providing evidence-based performance reporting. A vendor who compiles this manually from registers and estimates is providing anecdotal performance reporting.

Fleetcodes generates customer-specific performance reports automatically — OTDR, billing accuracy, POD compliance, exception rates — drawn from the operational record of every trip completed for that customer.

## The Vendor Qualification Process: What Happens in Practice

Enterprise shippers typically qualify logistics vendors through a formal process — a vendor questionnaire, a site visit, and sometimes a pilot period before awarding a full contract.

The vendor questionnaire typically asks:

- What GPS tracking system do you use and what refresh rate does it provide?
- Can you provide a customer-facing tracking portal?
- How are e-way bills generated — manually or integrated with your TMS?
- How are PODs captured — paper or digital?
- How quickly are invoices generated after delivery?
- How do you track vehicle document compliance?
- Can you provide performance reports by customer account?

A fleet operator running Fleetcodes can answer every question on this list with a specific, verifiable capability — not a claim, but a demonstrable system.

A fleet operator running manually can answer none of them satisfactorily.

The difference between competing on rate with small shippers and winning enterprise contracts on capability is significant in financial terms:
