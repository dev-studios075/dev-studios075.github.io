Fleetcodes applies machine learning and optimisation algorithms to the dispatch workflow in three distinct ways:

### 1. Intelligent Load-to-Vehicle Matching
When a new load enters the system, Fleetcodes doesn't just show a list of available vehicles. It analyses the load's requirements against the entire fleet — vehicle type, current location, current load status, driver hours available, vehicle maintenance schedule, and historical performance on similar routes — and surfaces the best match.

### 2. Route Optimisation at Scale
For fleets handling multiple pickups and deliveries per truck per day, manual route planning is a significant time sink — and rarely produces the optimal sequence. Fleetcodes' route engine calculates the most efficient sequence of stops for each truck, factoring in:

- Road conditions and traffic patterns
- Customer delivery windows
- Vehicle load capacity and sequence constraints
- Fuel efficiency of the specific vehicle on the specific route
- Real-time updates that reroute around delays

This is **operational efficiency** that compounds over thousands of trips — each individually optimised, collectively adding up to significant savings in fuel, time, and driver hours.

### 3. Predictive Dispatch Planning
Beyond reactive load matching, Fleetcodes uses historical trip data to help operations teams plan proactively. The system identifies patterns — routes that consistently run late, vehicles that consistently underperform on certain lane types, demand patterns that predict busy periods — and surfaces this intelligence for dispatchers to act on before problems occur.

This shifts dispatch from a reactive function (respond to today's loads) to a proactive one (anticipate tomorrow's requirements and position the fleet accordingly).

## The Manual Dispatch Problem at Scale

To understand why AI dispatch matters, consider what manual dispatch looks like at 100+ vehicles:

A dispatcher has a whiteboard (or a spreadsheet) showing vehicle locations, availability, and assignments. They receive a load booking. They mentally run through the available vehicles, check driver hours, think about the route, make a call.

This works when volumes are manageable. But as fleet size grows, the number of variables in every dispatch decision increases exponentially. A dispatcher managing 50 trucks is making hundreds of micro-decisions per day — and the quality of those decisions degrades with fatigue, time pressure, and information overload.

The result is:
- Suboptimal vehicle assignments that add unnecessary costs
- Routing decisions based on familiarity rather than optimisation
- Missed opportunities to fill empty return legs
- Delays in assignment that cause vehicles to sit idle during high-demand periods

**Fleet management shipping** operations that scale their dispatcher headcount in proportion to fleet size are solving the wrong problem. The right answer is **transport management system software** that makes each dispatcher exponentially more capable — not more dispatchers.
